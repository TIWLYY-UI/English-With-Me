🌟 Word of the Day
Persevere
verb /ˌpɜːsɪˈvɪə/
To continue in a course of action even in the face of difficulty or with little prospect of success.
"Despite many failures, she persevered and finally succeeded."

Phrasal Verbs

Common Phrasal Verbs

Phrasal verbs are a combination of a verb + preposition/adverb that create a new meaning.

get up
To rise from bed or a seat
"I get up at 6 am every day."
get along
To have a friendly relationship with someone
"She gets along well with her classmates."
get over
To recover from an illness or difficulty
"It took weeks to get over the cold."
look up
To search for information
"Look it up in the dictionary."
look forward to
To feel excited about a future event
"I look forward to seeing you."
look after
To take care of something or someone
"She looks after her younger brother."
take off
To leave the ground / remove clothing
"The plane took off at noon."
take over
To assume control of something
"She took over the project."
give up
To stop trying; to surrender
"Never give up on your dreams."
give away
To give something to others for free
"She gave away her old books."
put off
To postpone or delay something
"Don't put off what you can do today."
put up with
To tolerate something unpleasant
"I can't put up with the noise."
Idioms

Popular English Idioms

Idioms are phrases whose meaning cannot be understood from the individual words.

🌊
Break the ice
To initiate conversation in an awkward situation
"He told a joke to break the ice at the meeting."
🌧
Under the weather
Feeling unwell or sick
"I'm feeling a bit under the weather today."
🪣
Hit the nail on the head
To describe exactly what is causing a situation
"You've hit the nail on the head with that analysis."
🔥
Burn the midnight oil
To work or study late into the night
"She burned the midnight oil before the exam."
🎂
Piece of cake
Something that is very easy to do
"The test was a piece of cake for her."
🐟
Bite off more than you can chew
To take on more than you can handle
"He bit off more than he could chew with that project."
⛽
Add fuel to the fire
To make a bad situation worse
"His comments only added fuel to the fire."
🧱
Hit a brick wall
To reach a point where no progress is possible
"Negotiations hit a brick wall after hours of talks."
🌟
Once in a blue moon
Something that happens very rarely
"He only visits once in a blue moon."
